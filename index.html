What is Cloud Computing and Why Does It Matter for Businesses?

Cloud computing refers to the delivery of computing services — servers, storage, databases, networking, software — over the internet. Instead of owning physical infrastructure, businesses can rent access to computing resources from providers like AWS, Microsoft Azure, and Google Cloud.

For Rwandan businesses, cloud adoption means reduced infrastructure costs, improved collaboration, and the ability to scale operations quickly. During my certification in Emerging Technologies and Cloud Computing, I studied how these principles apply to real organisations operating in developing markets.

The key insight is that cloud computing is not just a technical concept — it is a business enabler that changes how teams work, how data is secured, and how organisations respond to change.

How Mobile Money is Reshaping Financial Inclusion in East Africa

Mobile money has become one of the most transformative financial technologies in East Africa. Platforms like MTN Mobile Money in Rwanda have enabled millions of people who previously lacked access to traditional banking to save, send, and receive money safely.

Working at MTN Rwanda has given me a frontline view of this impact. I speak with users every day who rely on Mobile Money for paying school fees, receiving wages, and running small businesses.

From an economic perspective, mobile money platforms are accelerating financial inclusion in ways that support Rwanda's Vision 2050 goals — directly aligned with my academic background in econometrics and statistical analysis.
